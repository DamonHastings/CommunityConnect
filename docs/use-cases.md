# CommunityConnect — Use Cases

---

## UC-1: Jason — Individual Seeking Resources and Stability

**Actor:** Jason, an individual experiencing homelessness and depression with very limited financial resources.

**Context:** Jason wants to find stability but doesn't know where to look for resources or support. A friend who discovered CommunityConnect suggests he sign up.

**Narrative:**
1. Jason's friend shares CommunityConnect with him as a place to find resources.
2. Jason signs up for a CC account.
3. After registering, Jason completes an intake questionnaire that captures his situation, needs, and goals.
4. Based on his responses, Jason receives a profile populated with all the service opportunities available to him.
5. From his profile, Jason can contact service providers directly.
6. Jason can apply for services and resources through the platform.
7. Jason can manage the services he's connected to — tracking status, follow-ups, and outcomes.

**Key Capabilities Required:**
- Individual user registration and profiles
- Intake questionnaire / needs assessment
- Need-based service and resource matching
- Service application workflow
- Individual service management dashboard
- Direct contact with service providers

## UC-2: Better Youth — Organization Creating and Running Programs

**Actor:** Better Youth (BY), a mentorship organization serving school-aged children.

**Context:** Better Youth runs in-school and community programs throughout the year, often collaborating with schools, other organizations, and individuals. They want a more streamlined way to build partnerships, create programs, receive applications, and increase their visibility.

**Narrative:**
1. Better Youth signs up for CommunityConnect as an organization.
2. BY creates an org profile, making them discoverable to potential partners and participants.
3. BY uses CC tools to create and publish programs.
4. BY uses outreach features to connect with potential partners (schools, other orgs, individuals).
5. Interested parties can discover BY and apply to their programs.
6. BY receives, reviews, and processes applications through the platform.
7. BY manages ongoing programs and partnerships through CC.

**Key Capabilities Required:**
- Organization profiles with discoverability
- Program creation and management
- Partner discovery and connection tools
- Application intake and processing workflow
- Outreach and visibility tools

## UC-3: Atnap — Business Seeking Organizational Clients

**Actor:** Atnap, a studio that provides business services to organizations.

**Context:** Atnap wants to reach potential clients within the community org space. They see CommunityConnect as a channel to become discoverable to the organizations on the platform.

**Narrative:**
1. Atnap creates a profile on CommunityConnect representing their studio and the services they offer.
2. Their profile is publicly visible and searchable by organizations on the platform.
3. Organizations browsing CC can discover Atnap and reach out to inquire about services.

**Key Capabilities Required:**
- Business/service-provider profile type (distinct from nonprofits/community orgs)
- Discoverability by organizations
- Profile with service offerings

## UC-4: Goings Academy — Institution Surfaced by AI Agent

**Actor:** Goings Academy, a school in the Sacramento area serving students who often need varying levels of social support.

**Context:** Goings Academy serves a demographic with diverse support needs. Rather than the school actively seeking out resources, CC's AI agent takes the initiative — profiling the school and connecting them to relevant resource and service providers.

**Narrative:**
1. Goings Academy exists as an entity within or connected to CC (as a partner, institution, or registered org).
2. CC's AI agent profiles Goings Academy — assessing their demographics, location, and likely needs.
3. The AI agent identifies relevant resource and service providers on the platform.
4. The AI agent proactively suggests or connects Goings Academy to those providers.

**Key Capabilities Required:**
- AI agent: institutional profiling
- AI agent: need inference and smart matching
- AI-driven outreach or suggestions to resource/service providers

## UC-5: Rachel Brown — Individual Professional Offering Pro Bono Services

**Actor:** Rachel Brown, a lawyer who provides pro bono legal counsel to underserved communities.

**Context:** Rachel wants to make her services discoverable so that organizations and individuals in need can find and connect with her.

**Narrative:**
1. Rachel creates an individual professional profile on CommunityConnect.
2. Her profile details her specialty (legal), the type of services she offers (pro bono counsel), and the communities she serves.
3. Organizations and individuals browsing CC can discover her profile.
4. Interested parties can contact Rachel through the platform.

**Key Capabilities Required:**
- Individual professional profiles (not org-affiliated)
- Discoverability by both organizations and individual users
- Specialty/service tagging for individuals
- Contact or inquiry mechanism

## UC-6: Better Youth + Wild Wild West — Joint Program

**Actor:** Better Youth (BY) and Wild Wild West (WWW), two organizations co-running a program.

**Context:** Better Youth is partnering with Wild Wild West to deliver a summer program for the youth BY serves. They want to coordinate the collaboration, seek funding, and manage applications — all on CC.

**Narrative:**
1. Better Youth initiates a joint program on CommunityConnect, inviting Wild Wild West as a co-organizer.
2. Both organizations collaborate on the program details, structure, and goals.
3. The joint program is listed on CC and is open for funding applications or grant-seeking.
4. Interested youth (or their guardians) and other stakeholders can apply to participate.
5. Both organizations receive and process applications together through the platform.
6. The program is conducted and managed through CC.

**Key Capabilities Required:**
- Joint/collaborative program creation with multi-org ownership
- Co-organizer invitation and shared program management
- Funding and grant application tooling
- Participant application intake and processing
- Shared program management dashboard
